# Scholarship Research for Mason — Rising Sophomore, Finance, Howard University (NJ resident)

Compiled July 2026. Prioritized: (1) Howard/HBCU-specific, (2) Black/African American business-finance, (3) New Jersey–based, (4) national finance/business for continuing students, (5) general national scholarships open to sophomores/juniors.

Every entry below was checked against live search results in July 2026. Where a program's exact 2026-2027 deadline wasn't visible in search results, the typical/recurring pattern is noted along with the source. **Because scholarship sites update frequently and some search snippets were incomplete, verify every deadline directly on the org's site before Mason invests time in an application.**

## CATEGORY 1: Howard University / HBCU-Specific

### 1. Thurgood Marshall College Fund (TMCF) — Open Scholarships Portal
Howard University is a confirmed TMCF member school, so Mason can create a TMCF Scholar profile and be auto-matched to dozens of corporate-sponsored scholarships (e.g., Wells Fargo Access Scholarship, Wells Fargo Gap Scholarship, Acushnet Company Scholarship) that rotate through TMCF's portal each cycle.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Must attend one of TMCF's ~55 member schools (public HBCUs, Howard included)
  - Individual sponsor scholarships vary in GPA (many require 2.5–3.0+), class year, and major requirements — Mason should filter by "business/finance" and "sophomore" once logged in
  - Must create a profile on the TMCF Scholarship Portal
- **Deadline:** 2026-2027 cycle: Wave 1 opened Sept 15, 2025, closed Jan 16, 2026 (already passed). Wave 2 opened Mar 9, 2026, closed Apr 24, 2026 (already passed). Additional scholarships were slated to open June 1, 2026 with rolling closes — check portal now for anything still open, and watch for the Fall 2026 wave announcement (new cycle typically opens ~September).
- **URL:** https://tmcf.org/scholarships/open-scholarships/ and https://scholarships.tmcf.org

### 2. American Water Impact Scholarship Program (formerly "Inclusion and Diversity Scholarship")
American Water funds $10,000 awards for students in non-medical STEM or business majors, with a specific track for students at HBCUs or Hispanic-Serving Institutions within its 23-state service footprint (New Jersey is one of the eligible states, both as home state and as an American Water operating state). **Caveat:** as of this search, American Water's page has been consolidated under "Impact Scholarship Program" branding and listed as "currently closed, 2026 program tentative" — the HBCU/diversity-specific framing may be in flux, so confirm current program structure before applying.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Enrolled or planning full-time undergraduate study in a non-medical STEM or business-related major
  - Minimum 3.0 GPA (Mason's 3.7 clears this easily)
  - Resides and/or attends college in an eligible state (list includes NJ)
  - HBCU/HSI track specifically targets students like Mason at Howard
- **Deadline:** Historically January 31 annually (2024 cycle deadline was Jan 31, 2024). 2026-2027 cycle status was listed as "closed/tentative" at time of research — watch for a fall/winter 2026 opening.
- **URL:** https://scholarshipamerica.org/scholarship/american-water/ (also indexed at https://learnmore.scholarsapply.org/american-water/)

### 3. Executive Leadership Council (ELC) Scholars Program
The ELC is a network of Black corporate executives that runs a flagship scholarship building a pipeline of Black business talent. This is a strong fit: it explicitly names "Rising Sophomore" as an eligible class year and prefers Business majors.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Attend an accredited 4-year college full-time
  - Minimum 3.0 GPA (Mason's 3.7 qualifies)
  - Classified as Rising Sophomore, Junior, Senior, or Graduate Student
  - Business, Engineering, and Technology majors preferred
  - Need-based component: requires FAFSA Submission Summary and tuition bill
  - Application includes essays, video prompts, business case responses, transcript, recommendation letter, resume, bio, and photo
- **Deadline:** March 1, 2026 for the current cycle (11:59pm EDT) — likely already closed for 2026; treat as an annual March 1 deadline and prepare early for 2027.
- **URL:** https://elcscholarships.elcinfo.com/ and https://www.elcinfo.com/our-impact/elc-scholarship-program/

## CATEGORY 2: Black/African American Students in Business, Finance, Banking, Economics

### 4. NABA National Scholarship Program (National Association of Black Accountants)
NABA's foundation funds 150+ scholarships a year ($2,500–$10,000) for Black/minority undergraduates in accounting, finance, or business. This is one of the best-matched programs for Mason given his major.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Ethnic minority undergraduate business major (accounting, finance, information systems, marketing, etc.) — finance qualifies
  - Must be a paid NABA member for the current fiscal year
  - Overall GPA of 3.3 required (accounting-specific GPA of 3.5 applies only to accounting majors) — Mason's 3.7 clears this
  - Full-time enrollment (12+ semester hours), freshman through first-year senior
  - Selection based on academic achievement, leadership, service, and engagement with NABA's mission
- **Deadline:** Recent cycle deadlines clustered around late June (June 29–30) with notifications by/around August for the Fall semester. Treat as a recurring early-summer deadline — check nabainc.org for the exact 2027 date.
- **URL:** https://nabainc.org/scholarships/

### 5. Congressional Black Caucus Foundation (CBCF) — CBC Spouses Education Scholarship
CBCF's flagship undergraduate award for academically talented Black/African American students, run by the spouses of Congressional Black Caucus members. Because Howard is in Washington, DC (Rep. Eleanor Holmes Norton's district, a CBC member), Mason should qualify via the "attends an academic institution in a CBC Member's District" criterion even though his home district in NJ may or may not have a CBC member.
- **Eligibility:**
  - U.S. citizen or legal permanent resident
  - Reside OR attend school in a CBC Member's congressional district (Howard/DC likely satisfies this)
  - Full-time enrollment in an undergraduate, graduate, or doctoral program
  - Minimum 2.5 GPA (Mason's 3.7 comfortably clears this)
  - Demonstrated leadership and community involvement
  - Application: 2-part online form, 3-minute video personal statement, 1 letter of recommendation, transcript, resume
- **Deadline:** 2026 cycle: opened Jan 5, 2026, closed March 27, 2026 (already passed this cycle). Recurring annual window is early January–late March — mark calendar for the CBCF's Fall 2026 announcement of the 2027 cycle.
- **URL:** https://www.cbcfinc.org/programs/scholarships/ (portal: https://cbcfinc.academicworks.com/)

### 6. National Black MBA Association (NBMBAA) Scholarship Program
NBMBAA funds undergraduate and MBA scholarships ($1,000–$10,000) for Black students in business fields, plus partner scholarships worth up to $50,000. **Note the eligibility gap:** search results indicate this program targets college juniors, seniors, and MBA students — as a rising sophomore, Mason likely isn't eligible yet, but this is worth bookmarking for his junior year (2027-2028).
- **Eligibility:**
  - Must be an NBMBAA member (paid membership)
  - College junior, senior, or MBA student (rising sophomore likely not yet eligible)
  - Pursuing a business-related bachelor's or graduate degree
  - Full-time enrollment, U.S. citizen or permanent resident
  - Community service/leadership involvement
- **Deadline:** 2026 cycle deadline was listed as May 29, 2026. Recurring roughly annual deadline in late May — revisit for junior year.
- **URL:** https://nbmbaa.org/colleges-scholarships/ and https://membership.nbmbaa.org/Programs/Scholarship-List/Scholarship-List

### 7. LeCount R. Davis, CFP® Endowed Scholarship (CFP Board Center for Financial Planning)
A scholarship specifically for Black/African American students pursuing CFP® certification, funded through the CFP Board. **Important caveat:** this requires enrollment in a "CFP Board Registered Program" — a specific financial-planning curriculum track, not just any finance major. Mason (or a parent) should check whether Howard's finance program has a CFP Board–registered concentration before applying; if not, this may not currently apply.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Self-identifies as Black or African American
  - Seeking to complete an undergraduate-level or certificate-level CFP Board Registered Program
  - Demonstrated merit and financial need
  - Committed to pursuing CFP® certification and a financial planning career
- **Deadline:** Not specified in search results — check CFP Board's scholarship page directly for the current cycle's opening/closing dates (these CFP Board scholarships often run on an annual application cycle in spring).
- **URL:** https://www.cfp.net/cfp-board-center-for-financial-planning/get-involved/ways-to-give/scholarships/lecount-davis-scholarship

### 8. SEO Career (Sponsors for Educational Opportunity)
Not a cash scholarship but a highly-regarded paid internship/professional development pipeline specifically for high-achieving Black, Hispanic, and Native American undergraduates targeting Wall Street, finance, and corporate careers — very relevant given Mason's major. Interns average $10,000 for the summer and ~80% convert to full-time offers.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Undergraduate student (rising sophomores through rising seniors typically apply, program targets students early enough to do 2+ summer internships)
  - Black, Hispanic, or Native American identity
  - Interest in finance, Wall Street, real estate, or related corporate tracks
  - Competitive application: resume, interviews, assessments
- **Deadline:** Rolling/cycle-based by industry track — check seo-usa.org for current application windows (SEO Career applications for summer internships typically open in the fall/winter prior).
- **URL:** https://www.seo-usa.org/our-programs/ and https://www.seo-usa.org/scholars/partners/internship-partners/

### 9. INROADS Internship Program
A paid internship (not a scholarship per se) placing Black, Hispanic, and Native American college students into corporate internships, with many finance/business placements. Useful complement to scholarship funding since Mason is now internship-eligible as a rising sophomore.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Full-time undergraduate, at least one summer remaining before graduation (fits Mason)
  - Minimum cumulative GPA of 2.8 (Mason's 3.7 far exceeds this)
  - All majors welcome; Business is a named target field
  - Requires INROADS-formatted resume, unofficial transcript, and completion of INROADS Interview Guidance and Preparation Session (IGPS)
- **Deadline:** Rolling/varies by region and corporate partner — apply directly at inroads.org for current openings.
- **URL:** https://inroads.org/internships-program/ and https://inroads.org/apply/

### 10. 100 Black Men of America — National Scholarship Program
Awards of $1,000–$5,000 to full-time college undergraduates. **Caveat:** eligibility appears restricted to 100 Black Men mentees, Collegiate 100® members, or program affiliates — Mason would likely need to join a local Collegiate 100 chapter or connect with a local 100 Black Men chapter (there may be one near his NJ hometown) to qualify.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Must be a 100 Black Men mentee, Collegiate 100 member, or affiliate
  - Graduating high school senior or full-time college undergraduate
  - Minimum 2.5 GPA (Mason qualifies)
- **Deadline:** Not specified in search results for the national program — varies by local chapter; check 100blackmen.org and local NJ chapter sites.
- **URL:** https://100blackmen.org/four-for-the-future/education/national-scholarship-program/

## CATEGORY 4: National Finance/Business/Economics Scholarships (Continuing Students)

### 13. Financial Planning Association (FPA) Diversity Scholarship
A merit-based scholarship for financial-services students who are actively promoting diversity within the financial planning profession — a plausible fit if Mason has done relevant coursework, clubs, or community work in financial literacy/planning.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Financial services student or professional
  - Must demonstrate active engagement in raising awareness of financial planning in diverse communities, or increasing professional opportunities for underrepresented groups in the field
  - Specific GPA/class-year requirements not detailed in search results — verify on FPA's site
- **Deadline:** Not specified for 2026-2027 in search results — recent recognitions occurred in September, suggesting a mid-year application cycle; confirm current dates directly.
- **URL:** https://www.financialplanningassociation.org/networking/diversity-equity-inclusion/diversity-scholarship

### 14. CFA Institute Student Scholarship
Not a cash scholarship, but a significant fee reduction (from ~$1,200+ down to $600) for the CFA Program exam registration for full-time or part-time university students. Useful if Mason plans to eventually pursue the CFA charter — worth knowing about now even if he won't sit for Level I as a sophomore.
- **Eligibility:**
  - Current full-time or part-time university student
  - Must meet CFA Program enrollment requirements
  - Not currently registered for a CFA exam, not awaiting results
- **Deadline:** Four application windows per year tied to each exam window (e.g., a Feb 2027 exam window opened May 5, 2026, closed May 25, 2026) — check cfainstitute.org for the next open window.
- **URL:** https://www.cfainstitute.org/programs/cfa-program/scholarships/student

## CATEGORY 5: General National Scholarships (Sophomores/Juniors, No Major Restriction)

### 15. Horatio Alger National Scholarship / Undergraduate Programs
A large, well-established scholarship organization ($25,000 awards) for students demonstrating financial need and resilience. **Caveat — verify carefully:** search results were ambiguous about whether currently-enrolled college sophomores can apply, versus only high school juniors/seniors "planning to pursue" a bachelor's degree. Horatio Alger does run 70+ distinct scholarship programs, some of which may target continuing college students, so it's worth checking horatioalger.org's undergraduate FAQ directly rather than assuming ineligibility.
- **Eligibility (main National Scholarship):**
  - Financial need: household adjusted gross income around $55,000–$65,000 or lower (varies by source — verify)
  - Minimum 2.0 GPA
  - U.S. citizen
  - Involvement in co-curricular/community service activities, demonstrated integrity/perseverance
- **Deadline:** Historically a Dec 1–March 1 (juniors) / Dec 1–Feb 15 (seniors) window — if a parallel continuing-student track exists, confirm its dates separately.
- **URL:** https://horatioalger.org/scholarships-and-services/undergraduate-scholarships/

### 16. Scholarships360 $10,000 No-Essay Scholarship
An easy-entry scholarship open to any student (high school through graduate/continuing ed) with no essay required and rolling monthly winners — low odds but zero-cost to enter and takes minutes.
- **Eligibility:** Any U.S. student, no essay, no GPA minimum specified
- **Deadline:** Rolling; 2026 cycle's final entry date was listed as June 30, 2026 — check for the next cycle's opening.
- **URL:** https://scholarships360.org/scholarships/no-essay-scholarships/

### 17. Sallie Monthly Scholarship (for College Sophomores)
A $2,000 monthly scholarship specifically open to college sophomores, no essay or profile required beyond a simple entry — directly matches Mason's current class year.
- **Eligibility:** Enrolled college sophomore, no essay required
- **Deadline:** Monthly rolling drawing — apply anytime
- **URL:** https://www.sallie.com/scholarships/states/new-jersey (state-specific hub) and https://www.sallie.com/scholarships

## Scholarships Investigated but NOT a Fit (excluded from list above — noted for transparency)

- **UNCF General Scholarship / PSE&G-UNCF Scholarship:** Requires enrollment at one of UNCF's 37 *member* private HBCUs. Confirmed via UNCF's own member-colleges page that **Howard University is NOT a UNCF member institution** (Howard is federally chartered, not one of the 37 private UNCF schools) — despite some secondary sources incorrectly implying otherwise. Mason is not eligible unless UNCF runs a separate non-member-school program later.
- **NJCPA Scholarship Foundation (NJ Society of CPAs):** Restricted to accounting majors/concentrations specifically (not finance) AND requires attendance at a New Jersey college — Howard (DC) and Mason's finance major both disqualify him.
- **PSEG Scholarship for Advancing STEM Majors:** Restricted to students at ICUNJ (Independent Colleges and Universities of NJ) member schools — Howard doesn't qualify, and it's STEM/engineering-focused, not finance.
- **NJ Utilities Association (James R. Leva Scholarship):** Restricted to NJUA member-company employees or their family members — only relevant if a parent/guardian works for an NJ utility.
- **Toigo Foundation Fellowship:** Confirmed graduate/MBA-only (second-year MBA students); not available to undergraduates. Worth revisiting after Mason completes his bachelor's if he pursues an MBA.
- **Jackie Robinson Foundation Scholarship:** Eligibility requires not having "accrued more than 25% of credits needed to graduate" — effectively limited to entering freshmen; a rising sophomore who completed a full first year likely exceeds this threshold.
- **Jack and Jill of America Foundation Scholarship:** Entry point is specifically "high school senior" (renewable in later years only for those who won as a HS senior) — not open to new applicants who are already sophomores.
- **Prudential Emerging Visionaries:** Age-restricted to 14–18 year-olds; Mason (college sophomore) is past the eligible age window.
- **Coca-Cola Scholars Foundation / Elks National Foundation MVS / Ron Brown Scholar Program:** All confirmed high-school-senior-only entry points.
- **NSBE (National Society of Black Engineers) Scholarships:** Engineering/applied-science focused, not a fit for a finance major.
- **National Black MBA Association:** Included above (#6) but flagged as likely requiring junior standing — not yet eligible as a rising sophomore.

- **Many 2026 deadlines shown above have already passed** as of this July 2026 research date (e.g., TMCF, CBCF, ELC, NJCPA all had winter/spring 2026 deadlines) — these are included because they are **real, currently-operating annual programs**, and the entries note the recurring deadline pattern so Mason can plan for the **next** cycle (typically opening between fall 2026 and spring 2027).
- A few programs (American Water, FPA Diversity Scholarship, LeCount R. Davis CFP Scholarship, Horatio Alger's undergraduate eligibility, 100 Black Men's local NJ chapter, HUAC-NJ) had incomplete deadline/eligibility detail in available search results — these are flagged individually and should be confirmed directly on the organization's site before Mason applies.
- The single most important correction from assumptions in the original research brief: **Howard University is not a UNCF member school**, which knocks out UNCF's General Scholarship and the PSE&G/UNCF NJ business scholarship — both would have otherwise looked like strong matches.
